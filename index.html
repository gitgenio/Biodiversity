Especies Endemicas

  • Ranita de Cristal Boyacense (Hyalinobatrachium durandi): This miniature glass frog, measuring a mere 17 millimeters in length, is a true gem of Boyacá's biodiversity. Its translucent skin allows its internal organs to be visible, earning it the name "glass frog."

  • Mono Titi de Cabeza Blanca (Callicebus caligatus): Adorned with a distinctive white cap, this charming primate is an endemic resident of Boyacá's cloud forests. Its long, bushy tail helps it navigate the dense treetops, while its vocalizations echo through the verdant canopy.

  • Frailejón de Boyacá (Espeletia boyacensis): This iconic páramo plant, with its rosettes of velvety leaves, is a symbol of Boyacá's high-altitude ecosystems. Adapted to the harsh páramo conditions, it plays a crucial role in water conservation and providing habitat for various páramo-dwelling creatures.

  • Hormiga Bala (Bala clavata): Renowned for its potent sting, this formidable ant species is endemic to Boyacá. Its large size and aggressive nature make it a formidable predator in the forest floor ecosystem.

  • Musgo de Barbas (Tillandsia usneoides): Commonly known as "musgo español," this plant drapes the branches of trees in Boyacá's cloud forests. Unlike most plants, it obtains its nutrients from the air and rain.
